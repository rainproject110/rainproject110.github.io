About RAIN-INN

Building Solutions Through Community

RAIN-INN is convened by Dr. Olusola Ayoola, founder of Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Nigeria (RAIN), which operates training facilities, research centers, and product development programs.

While RAIN offers paid professional training and certification, RAIN-INN was created to provide a community for those who wish to learn, collaborate, and build solutions but may not be able to access formal training programs.

This community brings together Nigerians and Africans from universities, polytechnics, secondary schools, companies, government agencies, NGOs, and the diaspora—all interested in using AI and robotics to solve real-world problems.

Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between RAIN and RAIN-INN?

RAIN (Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Nigeria) is a training facility, research center, and product development organization founded by Dr. Olusola Ayoola that offers paid professional programs. RAIN-INN is a free community initiative under RAIN, created for those who want to learn, collaborate, and build solutions but may not be able to access formal training programs.

Who can join RAIN-INN?

Anyone interested in AI and robotics solutions! Our community includes students from universities, polytechnics, and secondary schools; professionals from companies, government agencies, and NGOs; researchers; and diasporans across Nigeria, Africa, and worldwide.

Is there a membership fee?

No, RAIN-INN membership is completely free. The community was specifically created to be accessible to everyone, regardless of their ability to pay for formal training programs.

What are institutional clusters?

Clusters are sector-specific groups within RAIN-INN (e.g., healthcare, oil & gas, education, sports) where members discuss problems in their field and identify AI/robotics solutions. When skill gaps are identified, cluster members can access RAIN training to build the necessary capabilities.

How do hackathons and summits work?

Hackathons are regular collaborative events where community members work together to build solutions for real-world problems. Summits are annual gatherings where we showcase achievements, set research agendas, network, and establish focus areas for the coming quarter or year.

What is a RAIN-INN Ambassador?

Ambassadors represent RAIN-INN in their institutions, coordinate local activities and events, and serve as the communication bridge between community members at their institution and RAIN headquarters. They help ensure the community thrives at the grassroots level.

Do I need technical experience to join?

No! RAIN-INN welcomes people from all backgrounds and skill levels. Whether you're identifying problems that need solutions, funding development, or learning to build solutions yourself, there's a place for you in our community.

How is RAIN-INN managed?

RAIN-INN is convened by Dr. Olusola Ayoola and managed by a dedicated community manager. Clusters are coordinated from RAIN headquarters, and ambassadors help manage activities at the institutional level across the country.
